About the ICT Department

The ICT Department at Royal Crystal Academy is responsible for building our pupils' confidence and competence with technology — from early screen-time awareness in Nursery, through core computer literacy across Basic 1 to Basic 6, to a dedicated, opt-in ICT Practical Programme for Basic 3 – 6 pupils who want hands-on time beyond the regular class-wide ICT period.

ICT Vision and Objectives

Our ICT Vision

To raise digitally confident, responsible young learners who can use technology purposefully — as a tool for research, creativity, communication and problem-solving — well before they leave Basic 6 for secondary school.

Our Objectives

  • Introduce every pupil to computers in a safe, structured, age-appropriate way
  • Build practical keyboard, mouse and software skills progressively across the classes
  • Give Basic 3 – 6 pupils an optional deeper, hands-on practical track in coding and digital projects
  • Teach responsible, safe internet use and digital citizenship from an early age
  • Prepare graduating Basic 6 pupils for secondary school ICT / computer studies

Computer Laboratory Overview

Royal Crystal Academy is proud to operate a fully equipped ICT laboratory, giving every pupil from Basic 1 to Basic 6 hands-on access to computers as part of their regular learning. Our Nursery classes also enjoy supervised, screen-time-appropriate introductions to technology to build early familiarity before formal ICT lessons begin in Basic 1.

Each class has a scheduled weekly ICT period, supervised by our dedicated ICT Administrator, where pupils build practical digital skills alongside their core subjects.

Computer Lab Rules

  1. Always wait for the ICT teacher before touching any computer
  2. No eating or drinking inside the laboratory
  3. Report any fault or damage immediately
  4. Shut down computers properly at the end of each session
  5. Internet use is supervised at all times

ICT Curriculum for Basic 3 & Basic 4

The foundation years of the ICT Practical Programme — building real word-processing, spreadsheet and internet-safety skills.

Basic 3

Introduction to Word Processing

  • Opening and typing in a word processor
  • Formatting text — bold, font size, color
  • Saving and opening a file
  • Introduction to the internet (with supervision)
Basic 4

Spreadsheets & Internet Basics

  • Introduction to spreadsheets — rows, columns, simple data entry
  • Safe and supervised internet searching
  • Understanding email (concept-level)
  • Digital citizenship — staying safe online

ICT Curriculum for Basic 5 & Basic 6

The advanced years — presentations, block-based coding, and preparation for secondary school ICT.

Basic 5

Presentations & Intro to Coding

  • Creating simple slide presentations
  • Introduction to block-based coding (Scratch)
  • Basic computer troubleshooting awareness
  • Group ICT-based projects
Basic 6

Coding Fundamentals & Digital Projects

  • Block-based coding projects (Scratch) — games and animations
  • Introduction to typing-based coding concepts
  • Research and presentation skills using ICT tools
  • Preparation for secondary school ICT/computer studies

Practical Skills Pupils Will Learn

  • Confident typing and keyboarding
  • Word processing and document formatting
  • Basic spreadsheet data entry
  • Block-based coding with Scratch
  • Building simple slide presentations
  • Safe, supervised internet research
  • Digital citizenship & online safety
  • Basic computer troubleshooting
  • Teamwork through group digital projects
